Project Background: Is Pitchfork Biased?

To preface; the focus of this analysis is Pitchfork, a music media publication established in the late 90's in Chicago. What started out as a simple music website, grew exponentially in size, popularity and influence over the years, culminating in their acquisition by CondeÃÅ Nast in 2015. Pitchfork is best known for their comprehensive album reviews, which carry a significant amount of weight in the music industry as a whole. Pitchfork initially started as an indie publication, however the scope of their work grew along with the publication. From 2000 - 2017, Pitchfork published over 18,000 reviews, spanning 42 major sub-genres and more than 3,200 record labels.

Despite no longer being considered an indie website, there is a general consensus in the music industry that Pitchfork favors certain types of music due to its roots as an independent publication. The objective of this analysis is to investigate these lingering accusations of bias, and determine if there are patterns over the years which might signify favoritism towards certain genres, artists or music labels.

A dataset was created, comprised of the 17 years of available reviews, supplemented with additional information from Spotify and Wikipedia. The attributes from this dataset can be used to train a logistic regression classifier, returning the probability an album would receive a score which is above or below the yearly average score. From the derived model, feature influence could be examined by taking the log-likelihood of the coefficients to determine what impact the individual attribute had on the final probability.

A presentation outlining the results of the study can be viewed using the link below. Based on these results, this interactive app has been created, allowing a user to determine the probability of an album receiving an above average rating, solely based on the associated characteristics of the artist. The attributes which proved most important in making this determination include, related genres, Spotify streaming metrics, associated record label(s), and the tenure of the artist.
